BEST FOOD







Adobo

Adobo is a popular Filipino dish made with meat (often chicken or pork) marinated in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and spices, then simmered until tender. It's known for its savory and tangy flavor.

Sinigang

Sinigang is a sour soup native to the Philippines. It is characterized by its sour and savory taste, which is traditionally achieved by using tamarind as the souring agent.

Lechon

Lechon is a roasted pig dish popular in the Philippines. The whole pig is roasted over an open fire or in a pit until the skin is crispy and the meat is tender and flavorful.

Kare-Kare

Kare-Kare is a Filipino stew made with a thick savory peanut sauce. It is often cooked with various meats such as oxtail, tripe, and pork hocks, along with vegetables like eggplant, banana blossom, and string beans.

Pancit

Pancit is a Filipino noodle dish made with stir-fried noodles, meat, and vegetables. It comes in various regional variations and is often served on special occasions and gatherings.

Halo-Halo

Halo-Halo is a popular Filipino dessert that consists of shaved ice, various sweet ingredients such as beans, fruits, jellies, and ice cream, topped with evaporated milk and sometimes leche flan or ube halaya. It's a refreshing treat especially during hot days.
